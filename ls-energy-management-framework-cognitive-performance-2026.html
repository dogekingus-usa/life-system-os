2

Score Each Energy Dimension (30 min)

Rate yourself 1-10 on each of the four energy dimensions based on the last 30 days. Physical: sleep quality, exercise frequency, nutrition quality. Emotional: relationship satisfaction, boundary strength, recovery habits. Mental: focus quality, decision satisfaction, distraction frequency. Spiritual: purpose clarity, value alignment, sense of contribution.

3

Identify the Weakest Link (15 min)

The dimension with the lowest score is your bottleneck. Improving it will have the greatest impact on all other dimensions. For example, if physical energy scores a 4, no amount of meditation (spiritual) or focus apps (mental) will compensate for chronic sleep deprivation.

4

Map Energy Leaks (20 min)

List specific activities, habits, or environments that drain your energy across each dimension. Common leaks include: Physical: skipping meals, poor sleep hygiene. Emotional: toxic relationships, no boundaries. Mental: constant notifications, multitasking. Spiritual: work misaligned with values, no purpose clarity.

5

Design Your Energy-Matched Schedule (30 min)

Using your baseline data from Step 1, create a daily schedule that matches task types to energy levels. Deep work (creative, strategic, analytical) goes in your peak window. Administrative and routine work goes in your trough. Collaborative work goes in moderate energy periods. Block recovery time after peak windows.

6

Implement Recovery Rituals (15 min)

Design three specific recovery rituals: a mid-morning reset (10 min walk, deep breathing), a lunch break protocol (away from desk, proper meal, no screens), and an end-of-work transition (journal close-out, shower, music). These micro-recoveries prevent cumulative depletion.

7

Run a Weekly Review Tied to Energy (15 min)

At the end of each week, review: What tasks did I do during my peak energy window? What tasks leaked into my low-energy windows? Which energy dimension felt most depleted? Adjust your schedule for the following week based on this data.
