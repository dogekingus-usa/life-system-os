🧠 The Life OS Quick-Start Guide

Build your personal operating system in 4 pillars — and run your life on autopilot

"Your brain is for having ideas, not holding them." — David Allen
📥 Pillar 1: Capture System
  • Choose ONE inbox — Notes app, Notion, or physical notebook. Never more than one.
  • Capture in <30 seconds — Any thought, task, idea goes there immediately. No sorting yet.
  • Daily inbox-zero — Every evening, process all captured items: Delete, Delegate, Do, Defer.
  • Voice capture — Use Siri/Google voice notes for on-the-go captures (driving, shower).
📋 Pillar 2: Task Engine
  • One master task list — Todoist, Things, TickTick… pick ONE, stick with it.
  • Weekly review (Sunday) — 20 min. Clear inbox, review projects, plan top-3 for the week.
  • MIT method — Each day, define 3 Most Important Tasks. Do those before checking email.
  • Tag by context — @home, @office, @errand, @phone. Work on what fits your location.
🗓️ Pillar 3: Calendar & Rhythm
  • Time-block your week — Deep work (90 min blocks). Meetings (batched). Admin (30 min).
  • Theme your days — Monday = planning, Tue-Thu = deep work, Fri = admin/light tasks.
  • Buffer time — Block 20% of your calendar as unscheduled buffer. Life happens.
  • Energy-awareness — Schedule hard tasks at your peak energy time. Don't fight biology.
🔁 Pillar 4: Review & Improve
  • Daily 5-min closeout — What got done? What moved? What's tomorrow's MIT?
  • Weekly 20-min review — Inbox zero, calendar review, next week's priorities.
  • Monthly 1-hr audit — What's working? What drains energy? Kill 1 bad habit.
  • Quarterly reset — Goals check, system tune-up, tools audit. Keep what serves you.

The 2-Minute Rule: If a task takes ≤2 minutes, do it immediately. Don't add it to a list. Lists are for tasks, not micro-actions. This alone will save you 2+ hours per week.
