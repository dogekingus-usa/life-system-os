The Science of Habit Formation: How Long Until It Becomes Automatic?

Forming new habits can be challenging, but understanding the science behind habit formation can make it a lot easier. In this blog post, we'll explore how long it takes for a new habit to become automatic and provide actionable tips to help you build better habits faster.

The Stages of Habit Formation

Habit formation is often broken down into three stages: the initial phase, the consistency phase, and the automation phase. Each stage has its own challenges, but by understanding them, you can better manage your progress towards forming a new habit.

  • Initial Phase: This is when you first start trying to form a new habit. It's often challenging because you need to rely on willpower and motivation to keep going each day.
  • Consistency Phase: Once you've made it through the initial phase, you move into consistency. This stage involves sticking with your new habit regularly, even when you don't feel like doing so.
  • Automation Phase: Finally, as you repeat your new habit consistently over time, it becomes a part of your routine. At this point, the habit has become automatic, and you no longer need to think about performing it.

How Long Does It Take?

The exact timeframe for forming a new habit can vary widely from person to person. According to a study by Duke University, it takes an average of 66 days (about two months) to form a new habit, but this can range anywhere from 18 days to over 254 days.

Actionable Tips for Building Better Habits Faster

  • Start Small: Begin with a simple habit that you can easily stick to. For example, if your goal is to exercise more, start with a short walk each day before building up the duration and intensity.
  • Create a Routine: Tie your new habit to an existing one. For instance, brush your teeth and then floss, or after finishing work emails, stretch for five minutes.
  • Make It Visible: Place reminders around your home or office that will prompt you to stick with your new habit. This could be a post-it note on your fridge or an app notification reminding you of the task at hand.
  • Track Your Progress: Keep a journal or use an app to track your progress. Seeing your improvements over time can boost motivation and keep you on track.
  • Pick the Right Time: Choose a consistent time each day for your new habit. This helps build consistency and makes it more likely that the behavior will become automatic.

Conclusion

Forming habits takes time, but understanding the science behind it can help you make progress faster. By breaking down the process into stages, setting small goals, and staying consistent, you can turn your new habit into a lifelong routine.

Remember, forming a habit is not just about the initial effort; it's also about maintaining consistency over time. Keep pushing forward, even when things get tough, and soon enough, your new habits will become second nature.
