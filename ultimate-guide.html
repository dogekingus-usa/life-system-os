The 5 Pillars of a Life OS

1. Foundation: Core values, mission statement, long-term vision.
2. Daily Systems: Morning routines, time blocking, habit tracking.
3. Energy Management: Peak hours, strategic breaks, sleep optimization.
4. Weekly Review: Audit, adjust, celebrate wins, plan ahead.
5. Knowledge Management: Second brain, note-taking, continuous learning.

3

Step 1: Define Your Core Values

Start by writing down 3-5 core values that define who you want to be. Examples: Growth, Connection, Health, Creativity, Freedom. Every system you build should align with these values.

4

Step 2: Design Your Ideal Week

Use time blocking to map out your perfect week. Allocate 90-minute deep work blocks during peak energy hours. Schedule meetings in the afternoon. Leave buffer time between tasks.

5

Step 3: Build Daily Habits

Start with 3 keystone habits: Wake up at the same time, exercise for 20 minutes, and plan your day. Use habit stacking to attach new habits to existing ones.

6

Step 4: Create Your Second Brain

Set up a note-taking system (Notion/Obsidian) to capture ideas, projects, and learnings. Use the CODE method: Capture, Organize, Distill, Express.

7

Step 5: Weekly Review Ritual

Every Sunday, review your week: What worked? What didn't? What will you change next week? Update your systems accordingly. This is the secret to continuous improvement.

8

Tools to Get Started

Notion (all-in-one Life OS), Obsidian (second brain), Todoist (tasks), Google Calendar (time blocking), Motion (AI scheduling). Choose ONE and master it before adding more.
