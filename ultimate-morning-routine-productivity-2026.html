    Mistake 2: Trying to Do Everything

    The perfect morning routine is the one you actually do. If you're trying to meditate for 20 minutes, exercise for 45, journal for 15, read for 30, and still start work by 8 AM, you're setting yourself up for failure. Start with 2-3 core elements and add more only when the existing ones feel automatic.

    Mistake 3: No Pre-Morning Preparation

    Your morning routine actually starts the night before. If you're waking up to a messy kitchen, no idea what to wear, and no clarity on your priorities, you're adding decision fatigue to your morning before you've even started. Spend 10 minutes each evening preparing for the next morning.

    Mistake 4: Checking Your Phone First

    This is the most common and most damaging mistake. Every study on morning routines confirms that checking your phone within the first 30 minutes of waking reduces your productivity by 20-40% for the rest of the day. The solution is simple: keep your phone out of your bedroom, or use an analog alarm clock.

    Building Your Morning Routine in 7 Days

    • Day 3-4: Add 10 minutes of movement during the digital buffer.
    • Day 5-6: Add 5 minutes of journaling or priority setting.
    • Day 7+: Add the 90-minute deep work block before checking email or messages.
