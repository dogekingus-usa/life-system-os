3 Step 2: Create Your Master Dashboard Page

This is the home screen of your Life OS â€ the first thing you see every day. Design it as a clean, visually organized page with the following sections:

Create a "Morning Review" toggle with a checklist: "Review today's top 3 priorities," "Check calendar for meetings," "Log yesterday's habits," "Review weekly goal progress," and "Set intention for the day." This 5-minute ritual trains your brain to start each day with intention rather than reactivity.

Today's Tasks (Filtered View)

Embed a linked view of your Tasks database filtered to show only tasks due today or overdue, sorted by priority. Keep this view front and center â€ it's your daily action plan. Include a "Quick Add" button that opens a new task form with today's date pre-filled.

Weekly Goal Progress

Show a compact view of your current quarterly goals with progress bars. Use Notion's formula capabilities to show percentage complete based on completed key results or linked task completion rates. Seeing progress visually is a powerful motivator.

Habit Tracker (This Week)

Embed a gallery or board view of your habits for the current week. Use checkbox properties for each day. Color-code completed days green and missed days red for instant visual feedback. A streak counter below each habit gamifies the process and keeps you consistent.

Quick Links

Add a row of linked buttons for your most-used pages: Weekly Review page, Monthly Planning page, Journal, Reading List, and Finances Dashboard. This reduces friction and keeps you moving fast.

Design Tip: Use Notion's callout blocks, dividers, and colored headings to create visual separation between dashboard sections. A well-designed dashboard is one you'll actually use every day. Add a subtle background image or gradient for personality.
