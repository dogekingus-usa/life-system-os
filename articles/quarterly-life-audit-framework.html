How to Conduct a Quarterly Life Audit: Reflect, Recalibrate, Recommit

Published: May 20, 2026 | 9 min read | Category: Life OS Framework

A quarterly life audit is your personal board of directors meeting. Every 90 days, you step back from the day-to-day to assess whether you're heading in the right direction. Companies do quarterly reviews as a matter of survival. You should too. Here's the complete framework.

Why Quarterly? Why Not Monthly or Yearly?

Yearly reviews are too infrequent too much time passes before you course-correct. Monthly reviews are too frequent not enough time passes to see meaningful trends. Quarterly is the sweet spot. Ninety days is long enough to make real progress but short enough to catch drift before it becomes a crisis. Four times per year is enough to create momentum without creating overwhelm.

Preparing for Your Audit: Gather Your Data

Spend 15 minutes collecting information before your audit:

  • Your calendar from the last 90 days
  • Your task manager (completed and pending items)
  • Your journal or notes (if you keep them)
  • Your goal list from the beginning of the quarter
  • Your financial statements (income, expenses, net worth)
  • Your health data (sleep, exercise, any relevant metrics)

The 4-Question Audit Framework

Question 1: What Did I Accomplish?

List your wins from the past 90 days. Be specific. These don't need to be huge finishing a book, starting a new habit, having an important conversation. Write at least 10 wins. This question builds momentum and proves to yourself that you're moving forward, even when it doesn't feel like it.

Question 2: What Didn't Go Well?

Be honest here. What goals did you abandon? What habits fell off? Where did you waste time? This is not a guilt exercise it's a diagnostic. Without identifying what didn't work, you'll repeat the same patterns next quarter.

Question 3: What Did I Learn?

Extract lessons from both your wins and failures. What surprised you? What would you tell your past self? What patterns emerge? This is the most important question learning is the compound interest of personal growth.

Question 4: What Will I Change Next Quarter?

Based on your answers above, identify 1-3 specific changes for the next 90 days. Not goals changes. What will you START doing? What will you STOP doing? What will you CONTINUE doing but differently?

The One-Question Audit: If you only have 10 minutes, ask one question: "If I could redo the last 90 days, what would I change?" The answer reveals your most important insight.

The Life Pillar Scorecard

Rate each pillar of your life (Health, Wealth, Work, Relationships, Growth, Purpose) on a scale of 1-10 for the quarter. Then compare to last quarter's scores. The delta (change) matters more than the absolute number:

  • Up 2+ points: What worked? Double down on it next quarter.
  • Unchanged: This area is stable. Is that acceptable, or does it need attention?
  • Down 2+ points: What caused the drop? Is this a temporary blip or a trend?

Goal Progress Review

Review each goal you set at the beginning of the year or quarter:

  • Completed: Celebrate and archive. Set a new goal if appropriate.
  • On track: What's working? Keep doing it.
  • Behind: Is the goal still relevant? If yes, what's blocking progress? If no, revise or abandon it.
  • Abandoned: That's okay. Not every goal survives contact with reality. The question is: was abandoning it the right decision?

The Quarterly Commitment Ceremony

End your audit by writing down three commitments for the next quarter:

  • One goal you will achieve
  • One habit you will build
  • One thing you will stop doing

Sign and date this page. This creates accountability. In 90 days, you'll look back and see if you kept your commitments to yourself.

How to Schedule Your Quarterly Audit

Block 2 hours on the last Sunday of each quarter. No exceptions. Treat it like a board meeting because it is one. You are the CEO, the board, and the shareholder of your own life. The quarterly audit is how you ensure the company (you) is heading in the right direction.

Next audit dates: Last Sunday of June (mid-year), September, December, and March. Put them on your calendar now for the entire year. Future you will be grateful.
